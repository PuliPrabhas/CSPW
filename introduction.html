Introduction

The Introduction is as Follows

The identification of people preferring for Shopping is an essential part of everyday life, and consumers can choose between online and offline shopping based on their preferences and needs. Offline shopping refers to purchasing products in physical stores, allowing customers to see, touch, and try items before buying. It provides a personalized shopping experience with direct customer service. On the other hand, online shopping offers convenience, a wider selection of products, and the ability to shop anytime from anywhere. Both shopping methods have their advantages and challenges, and consumer choices are influenced by factors such as convenience, product availability, pricing, and personal preferences.
