Based on the survey of 50 respondents, 30 individuals preferred offline shopping while 20 chose online platforms. Flipkart emerged as the top choice online (7 respondents), and Westside led offline (7 respondents).

The data indicates a general preference for offline retail, likely owing to the tactile experience, product availability, and trust in traditional stores. Online shopping remains popular for its convenience, though dominated by a few major platforms. Offline channels are more diverse, with interest distributed across several stores.

These findings highlight that, despite the growing digital trend, brick-and-mortar shopping continues to hold strong relevance. Both modes have loyal user bases, underscoring the need for businesses to maintain a balanced omnichannel presence.
